How to Carve a Pumpkin



Halloween is just around the corner. Everyone’s setting up their old decorations, and streets are painted with bright orange jack-o-lanterns. In this article, you will learn how to carve your very own pumpkin into a spooky jack-o-lantern.

Here are the steps to carve your pumpkin:



Pre-Carving Steps


  1. It’s crucial to pick a good pumpkin to compliment the type of face you would like to carve onto your pumpkin. When you’re at the supermarket, choose a large pumpkin with lots of surface area for your imagination to run wild.

  2. While at the store, you may pick up a pumpkin carving set. A simple $5 kit will suffice.

  3. Wash your pumpkin to remove any excess pathogens and wipe it down with a paper towel when you get home.

  4. Think of a face you like and draw the picture of your design onto the pumpkin with a black marker, or trace a stencil.

  5. Use the given knife in the pumpkin carving kit to cut out and put aside a circular lid from the top of the pumpkin and use a large spoon (or your hands) to scoop out the flesh.




Jack-o-Lantern (Mathias P.R. Reding) - https://unsplash.com/photos/L4CCJXGSK8I



Carving Steps


  1. Carve out each of your jack-o-lantern components, one at a time, and pop the bits out with your finger.

  2. Remember to carve steadily to avoid messing up your design and prevent any injuries.


Finishing Up


  1. To be safe, place an electric light in the centre of the base of your pumpkin as a flame may catch the pumpkin on fire which can be hazardous.

  2. Put the lid back on top of the pumpkin to encapsulate the light.

  3. Place the fully carved jack-o-lantern in your desired spot to compliment all your other Halloween decorations.


Final Tips


  1. To extend the lifespan of your pumpkin, keep it out of direct sunlight to slow decomposition.

  2. Pick a smooth and large pumpkin to extend your canvas space when selecting a pumpkin.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully carved your very own jack-o-lantern to add to your Halloween decoration gallery. Have a spooky Halloween!
